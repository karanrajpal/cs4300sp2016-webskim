    N.H.L. This Week: Playoff Spots at Stake as Season Winds Down

    By NAILA-JEAN MEYERSAPRIL 5, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    With the N.H.L. regular season in its final week, The New York Times looks ahead to the playoffs, highlighting hot — or not so hot — teams and players. (All times Eastern.)

    Games to Watch

    Panthers at Canadiens, Tuesday, 7:30 p.m.

    John Scott’s exile to Newfoundland is over for now. The unlikely All-Star Game most valuable player was called up from the American Hockey League to play one game for Montreal — “unless I go out and maybe score a hat trick,” Scott said jokingly Monday. The Canadiens acquired Scott as a part of a trade after he had been voted as an All-Star and promptly sent him to the minor leagues.

    “Since he joined the organization, he went to St. John with a great attitude,” Canadiens Coach Michel Therrien told reporters. “He was really good for the young kids over there.”

    He added, “The idea is we want to show respect because he’s been a real pro since he joined us and we want to give him the opportunity to play a game with the Montreal Canadiens.”

    Avalanche at Predators; Wild at Sharks

    Colorado is imploding, so Coach Patrick Roy is exploding. Roy lashed out at his players Sunday, saying, “Our core hasn’t proved that they have the leadership to bring this team to another level.” With a shot at the eighth and final playoff spot in the West, the Avs have lost three in a row and six of 10. Heading into Tuesday’s 8 p.m. game in Nashville, Colorado trails Minnesota by 5 points and will be eliminated with a regulation loss or a win by the Wild, who host San Jose at the same time.

    Flyers at Red Wings, Wednesday; Red Wings at Bruins, Thursday

    A playoff spot is there for the taking for Detroit, which has back-to-back games against its nearest rivals in the standings. With three games to play, the Red Wings are a point ahead of the Bruins for third place in the Atlantic Division, and also hold the tiebreaker — for now — over the Flyers, should the second wild-card spot come into play. Victories over both teams would give Detroit some breathing room as it attempts to qualify for the playoffs for the 25th season in a row.

    Islanders at Rangers, Thursday, 7 p.m.

    The Rangers clinched a playoff berth on Monday night, but lost their top defenseman, Ryan McDonagh, to a hand injury in the process. The Islanders can punch their postseason ticket with a win Tuesday at Washington. The Rangers are 2 points ahead of the Isles for third place in the Metropolitan Division, but neither team is particularly sharp as their final meeting of the season approaches. A first-round playoff series between the teams, once thought to be inevitable, is a long shot now, but the Islanders have bragging rights this season, winning the previous three games. The “reward” for finishing third in the division is a playoff matchup against the Penguins, the hottest team in the league.

    Ducks at Kings, Thursday, 10:30 p.m.

    The Pacific Division title is likely to be on the line when the Southern California rivals meet for the last time in the regular season. Anaheim came into Tuesday’s action with a 1-point lead over Los Angeles and had won three of its first four games against the Kings this season.

    Capitals at Blues, Saturday, 8 p.m.

    This could be a matchup of the No. 1 seed in the East vs. the No. 1 seed in the West. Washington claimed the top spot in the East long ago, but St. Louis has won seven of eight, including a 4-0 victory over the Capitals on March 26, to make a run in the West. With 105 points and two games left, the Blues are tied with the Dallas Stars atop the conference, but do not own the tiebreaker. They also learned Monday that their captain, David Backes, and goaltender Jake Allen would miss the rest of the regular season, at least, with injuries. Even if the game does not end up being a clash of No. 1 seeds, T.J. Oshie is sure to receive a warm welcome in his return to St. Louis. He missed the teams’ other game this season because of flulike symptoms.

    Flyers at Islanders, Sunday, 7 p.m.

    The Eastern Conference playoff matchups may not be settled until the Flyers and the Islanders complete their business on Sunday night. That is probably not what the N.H.L. expected when they scheduled this makeup game for a Jan. 23 contest that was postponed by a blizzard. On Jan. 23, Philadelphia was 20-17-8 and in 11th place in the East.

    N.C.A.A. Men’s Frozen Four, Thursday

    Quinnipiac, which has been ranked No. 1 for much of the season, is taking aim at its first national championship at Amalie Arena in Tampa, Fla. The Bobcats face Boston College in the first semifinal at 5 p.m. (ESPN2). The Eagles won the title the last time the Frozen Four was held in Tampa, in 2012. The North Dakota Fighting Hawks are in the Frozen Four for the third year in a row, but have not reached the final since 2005. That year, North Dakota lost to Denver, which just so happens to be its opponent Thursday. The final is Saturday at 8 p.m. on ESPN.

    In Case You Missed It

    About an hour after Kris Jenkins beat the buzzer and lifted Villanova to a men’s basketball championship, another tight title fight was settled by a late score. Alex Carpenter, the Boston College star, scored 12 minutes 30 seconds into overtime to lift the United States to a 1-0 victory over Canada at the women’s world championship in Kamloops, British Columbia. Led by goalie Alex Rigby, the Americans allowed only two goals in five games.

    The United States has won three world championships in a row and seven of nine. The United States and Canada have met in all 17 finals since the tournament began in 1990.

    Get to Know

    Penguins goaltender Matt Murray

    Pittsburgh is surging, winning 11 of 12, even in the face of key injuries. Evgeni Malkin has been out since March 12, and the Penguins lost goalie Marc-Andre Fleury to a concussion last week.

    Turning to a rookie goaltender late in the season is not an ideal situation, but Matt Murray has filled in admirably.

    Selected in the third round of the 2012 draft by Pittsburgh, Murray had a scoreless streak of 304:11 for Wilkes-Barre of the American Hockey League last season.

    He made his N.H.L. debut on Dec. 19 and has compiled a 7-2-1 record, a .933 save percentage and a 1.88 goals-against average since. A 21-year-old from Thunder Bay, Ontario, Murray has won his last five starts, including a shutout of the Islanders, but has been aided by 25 Pittsburgh goals in those games.

    “You kind of want to be the go-to guy,” Murray said Saturday. “Personally, I really don’t like sitting on the bench, especially for long periods of time, kind of like I have been lately. So this is a great opportunity for me.”

    The Penguins can measure themselves against the league-leading Capitals on Thursday night in Washington, before wrapping up the regular season Saturday in Philadelphia against the archrival Flyers, who are clinging to a playoff spot.
