    Iona Lands N.C.A.A. Berth With Upset of Monmouth

    By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 7, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    A. J. English hit a fadeaway jumper with 1 minute 39 seconds left to break a tie, Deyshonee Much followed with a driving layup that he converted into a 3-point play, and second-seeded Iona beat top-seeded Monmouth, 79-76, in Albany on Monday night to win the Metro Atlantic Athletic Conference tournament title and an N.C.A.A. tournament berth.

    Monmouth (27-7), which joined the MAAC in 2013, was seeking its first tournament appearance since 2006. It entered the title game on a five-game winning streak and had won 13 of 14 in a storybook season, the lone loss having been at Iona (22-10).

    HOFSTRA FALLS IN C.A.A. FINAL North Carolina Wilmington (25-7) won its first Colonial Athletic Association title in 10 years and earned a trip to the N.C.A.A. tournament, getting 20 points from Craig Ponder in an 80-73 overtime victory over top-seeded Hofstra (24-9) in Baltimore.

    STONY BROOK ADVANCES Carson Puriefoy scored 22 points, Jameel Warney added 21, and top-seeded Stony Brook (25-6) beat No. 7 seed Hartford (10-23), 80-64, in an America East Conference tournament semifinal in Stony Brook, N.Y. In the title game Saturday, the Seawolves will face third-seeded Vermont, which defeated New Hampshire, 63-56, in Burlington, Vt.

    SOUTHERN CONFERENCE TITLE Greg Pryor and Tre’ McLean each scored 13 points, and top-seeded Chattanooga (29-5) held on to defeat second-seeded East Tennessee State (23-11), 73-67, in Asheville, N.C., to win the Southern Conference championship and earn an automatic bid to the N.C.A.A. tournament.

    WISCONSIN COACH TO STAY ON Wisconsin named the interim coach Greg Gard as the permanent replacement for the retired Bo Ryan.

    Women

    UCONN WINS A.A.C. TITLE Breanna Stewart scored 22 points and blocked 7 shots as top-ranked Connecticut (32-0) beat No. 21 South Florida (23-9), 77-51, in Uncasville, Conn., to win its third straight American Athletic Conference championship.

    The freshman Katie Lou Samuelson added 13 points and a season-high 7 rebounds for the Huskies, who extended the third-longest streak in team and N.C.A.A. women’s Division I history to 69 games.

    IONA TAKES MAAC CROWN Marina Lizarazu scored 19 points, and Joy Adams added 11 points and 13 rebounds to help Iona (23-11) earn its first N.C.A.A. tournament trip with a 57-41 victory over Quinnipiac (24-8) in the MAAC championship game in Albany.

    BIG EAST SEMIFINALS Aliyyah Handford scored 18 of her 23 points in the second half, Danaejah Grant added 22 points and 10 rebounds, and No. 4 seed St. John’s (22-9) beat the top seed, No. 18 DePaul (25-8), 75-66, in the semifinals of the Big East Conference tournament in Chicago.

    In the final, St. John’s will meet seventh-seeded Creighton (17-16), which defeated third-seeded Seton Hall (23-8), 77-56. Creighton’s M. C. McGrory led all scorers with 23 points.

    BAYLOR CAPTURES BIG 12 Nina Davis scored 22 points to help No. 4 Baylor (33-1) beat No. 7 Texas (28-4), 79-63, in the Big 12 tournament championship game in Oklahoma City.
