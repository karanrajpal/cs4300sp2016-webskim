    Wings Lose to Rangers but Make Playoffs

    By DAVE CALDWELLAPRIL 9, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    The grizzled Detroit Red Wings forward Brad Richards could not recall any of his first 16 N.H.L. regular seasons ending quite like this: with his teammates gathered in front of a television in a road team’s dressing room waiting for an also-ran squad playing 215 miles away to polish off a blowout.

    That team was the Ottawa Senators, who routed the Boston Bruins on Saturday to guarantee the Red Wings one of eight Eastern Conference berths in the Stanley Cup playoffs. The Red Wings lost to the Rangers at Madison Square Garden, 3-2, but that meant nothing.

    Detroit (41-30-11) qualified for the playoffs for the 25th straight season, the longest current such streak in the N.H.L., and defenseman Danny DeKeyser acknowledged after Saturday’s game that during the last month the streak had been “in your head a little bit.”

    But the Red Wings survived because Boston (42-31-9) stumbled badly against Ottawa at home, and Richards said his teammates were excited to make it, though far from exultant.

    “There’s nothing that’s really been accomplished, other than that you’re going to be playing another two weeks for sure,” Richards said.

    Richards, 35, is with his third team in three seasons. After the Rangers bought out his contract in 2014, he played for one year with the Chicago Blackhawks, helping them to their third Stanley Cup in six seasons and to the second Cup in his career.

    Richards signed a one-year, $3 million contract with the Red Wings this year, and it appeared he might miss the playoffs for the first time since he played for Dallas in 2011.

    But the Red Wings beat the Philadelphia Flyers on Tuesday, 3-0, to help put themselves in good shape.

    “I’m just happy I have another chance,” Richards said after Saturday’s game.

    Detroit finished third in the Atlantic Division and will play the Tampa Bay Lightning, who will be without the injured Steven Stamkos, in the first round of the playoffs. The Red Wings lost to the Lightning in seven games in a first-round playoff series last year. Tampa Bay lost to Chicago in the finals.

    Two and a half hours after falling to the Senators, 6-1, the Bruins were eliminated from playoff contention when the Flyers beat Pittsburgh, 3-1. The Flyers (40-27-14) clinched the second and final wild-card berth in the Eastern Conference.

    The Red Wings would have made the playoffs simply by beating the Rangers (46-27-9), who sat goaltender Henrik Lundqvist and forwards Mats Zuccarello and Viktor Stalberg for the season finale. Defensemen Ryan McDonagh and Dan Girardi were out with injuries.

    “We had good chances to find the back of the net, but we just have to send our thanks to Ottawa,” Detroit forward Henrik Zetterberg said after his team lost.

    After an apparent first-period goal by Joakim Andersson was waved off because he was offside, the Rangers took a 1-0 lead on defenseman Dan Boyle’s 10th goal. But Detroit tied the score at 6 minutes 42 seconds of the second period on a power-play deflection by Riley Sheahan.

    Kevin Hayes gave the Rangers the lead again by tipping a shot past goaltender Jimmy Howard with 5:38 left in regulation, and Rangers forward Oscar Lindberg scored into an empty net with 2:36 remaining.

    Detroit’s Justin Abdelkader scored 62 seconds later to cut the Rangers’ lead to 3-2. Boyle picked up a crosschecking penalty with 1:13 left and Marc Staal tripped Zetterberg with 20 seconds left. With Howard pulled again for an extra attacker, the Red Wings had a six-on-three advantage.

    But Detroit did not get a shot on goal, let alone a game-tying goal. It was no great loss.

    “I know, in one-game deals, today it was potentially, you could play one good game and lose,” said Jeff Blashill, Detroit’s first-year coach. “That’s why you bank as many points as you can. We had the points, and the tiebreaker, to earn our way into the playoffs.”

    The Rangers, who could face Pittsburgh or Florida in the first round of the playoffs, topped 100 points for the second year in a row — the first time they have done that since 1973. The veteran forward Eric Staal left the game in the second period after colliding with his teammate Jesper Fast, but Coach Alain Vigneault expected Staal to be fine.

    “We knew going into the game there was a lot more on the line for them than there was for us,” Rangers defenseman Marc Staal said. “We approach it like we want to play our game, and our system was structured, and we had some guys coming in who contributed.”

    The Red Wings are accustomed to playing well into April, but this will be the first Stanley Cup playoff appearance for their 19-year-old rookie forward Dylan Larkin, who grew up in the Detroit suburbs, played at Michigan and scored 23 goals this season.

    Larkin said he would not be convinced that Detroit was in the playoffs until he saw the “X” next to the team’s name in the standings. But he did sound as if he were already thinking ahead.

    “The only thing I’m not looking forward to is my playoff beard,” Larkin said.
