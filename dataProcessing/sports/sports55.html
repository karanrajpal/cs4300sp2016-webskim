    Pacers Rout Nets to Make Playoffs; Three Battle On in West

    By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 10, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    Myles Turner had 28 points and 10 rebounds and the Indiana Pacers blew out the visiting Nets, 129-105, on Sunday to clinch a playoff berth.

    Solomon Hill had 13 points and 12 rebounds for Indiana, and George Hill had 18 points, Ian Mahinmi had 16 and Paul George 15. The Pacers led by as many as 42 points.

    The Pacers can wind up as high as seventh in the Eastern Conference if they finish with the same record as Detroit.

    Sean Kilpatrick led the Nets with 26 points.

    JAZZ 100, NUGGETS 84 Gordon Hayward and Trey Lyles each had 22 points as Utah won at Denver and maintained a slim lead for the last playoff spot in the Western Conference.

    The win kept the Jazz in eighth place, one game ahead of Houston and one game behind Dallas in a three-team race for two playoff spots.

    Continue reading the main story

    Rudy Gobert had 16 points, 14 rebounds and 6 blocks for the Jazz, who host Dallas on Monday.

    Nikola Jokic and Emmanuel Mudiay each had 19 points for the Nuggets.

    ROCKETS 130, LAKERS 110 James Harden had 40 points and 13 assists as Houston kept its playoff hopes alive with a win over visiting Los Angeles.

    Trevor Ariza and Patrick Beverley each scored 19 points for the Rockets, who had lost two straight.

    The Lakers trailed by 2 early in the fourth quarter before Harden scored 16 points in less than four minutes.

    Kobe Bryant had 35 points for the Lakers.

    CLIPPERS 98, MAVERICKS 91 Jamal Crawford scored 22 points and host Los Angeles beat Dallas, preventing it from clinching a playoff berth.

    Blake Griffin had 17 points and 11 rebounds in his best game since he returned last week from a three-month absence for the playoff-bound Clippers.

    Raymond Felton scored 21 points for the Mavericks, who are seeking their 15th playoff berth in 16 years.

    WIZARDS 113, HORNETS 98 Marcin Gortat had 21 points and 13 rebounds as Washington won at Charlotte and damaged the Hornets’ chances for home-court advantage in the first round of the playoffs.

    The Hornets trail the Celtics, the No. 4 team in the conference, by a game and play at Boston on Monday night.

    BUCKS 109, 76ERS 108 Khris Middleton scored 36 points to lead Milwaukee to an overtime win at Philadelphia.

    Before the game, the Sixers announced the hiring of Bryan Colangelo as team president. His father, Jerry, gave up his role as chairman of basketball operations but will stay on as a special adviser.

    HEAT 118, MAGIC 96 Luol Deng scored 20 points, Hassan Whiteside added 18 points and 15 rebounds in his first start since Jan. 20, and Miami beat visiting Orlando.
