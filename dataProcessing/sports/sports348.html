    Cal Clutterbuck Churns Out Big Goals for Islanders

    By ALLAN KREDAMARCH 30, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    EAST MEADOW, N.Y. — Cal Clutterbuck and John Tavares were linemates for two seasons a decade ago when they were playing junior hockey for the Oshawa Generals.

    The teenage Tavares was a sensation, scoring 72 goals in the 2006-7 season on his way to becoming the top overall pick by the Islanders two years later. Clutterbuck was an offensive force, too, scoring 35 goals for the second straight season and adding eight playoff goals, one more than Tavares.

    Now a gritty and resourceful leader for the Islanders, Clutterbuck is scoring key goals at a pace reminiscent of his teenage years. Five of his 15 goals have been game-winners, including an overtime goal against Carolina last Saturday, a late third-period score that completed a three-goal comeback against Florida on March 14, and a tiebreaking tally that defeated the Rangers at Madison Square Garden on March 6.

    Clutterbuck’s hard-nosed play alongside his linemates, Casey Cizikas and Matt Martin, often proves to be a difference-maker for the Islanders, who will host the Columbus Blue Jackets on Thursday.

    “Whenever I get the chance to shoot, I’m just trying to put it on net; I like to shoot the puck,” said Clutterbuck, who was acquired from the Minnesota Wild in a 2013 draft-day trade for Nino Niederreiter. “I wish I had a better answer. I just try to be consistent.”

    The Islanders surely need more of what Clutterbuck has generated as they work to secure a playoff spot for the third time in four seasons. Heading into Thursday’s game, they held the seventh of eight playoff spots in the Eastern Conference. Where they finish could come down to the season finale at Barclays Center against the Philadelphia Flyers on April 10.

    Clutterbuck brings a physical presence to each shift, though he has only 20 penalty minutes, 16 fewer than Tavares.

    Tavares, the Islanders’ captain, marvels at the success of Clutterbuck, who scored a career-best 19 goals for the Wild in 2010-11.

    “He has confidence shooting pucks, making plays and producing offense,” said Tavares, who has five game-winning goals, tied with Clutterbuck for the team lead, among his 27 scores. “It’s no secret Cal has one of the best releases on our team.”

    Clutterbuck skates with controlled abandon, pinballing against opposing skaters and causing general havoc. His line’s rollicking play set the tone in playoff games last season.

    “I played against Cal and he’s tough to play against,” said Frans Nielsen, who was a rookie with the Islanders when Tavares and Clutterbuck were scoring in bunches for Oshawa. “He’s got a good shot, he skates hard and he hits harder than anyone in this league.”

    Clutterbuck prefers to accentuate the contribution of teammates when discussing his play.

    “The time of the game doesn’t really dawn on you in the moment, it’s just kind of happening,” said Clutterbuck, a native of Welland, Ontario, who was selected by the Wild in the third round of the 2006 draft. “When you get chances on a regular basis, you feel better. Credit for that goes to Marty and Casey, to the trust I have with them.”

    Islanders Coach Jack Capuano lauded Clutterbuck after his overtime goal lifted the Islanders to a 4-3 win in Raleigh last Saturday, a crucial win for a team missing its starting goaltender, Jaroslav Halak, to a lower-body injury. Clutterbuck had also scored to tie the game at 3-3 early in the third period.

    “Clutter came up big; he’s a leader for us,” Capuano said. “He shoots the puck well, was in the right spot, and we got a bounce that we deserved.”

    Cizikas emphasized that Clutterbuck’s fiery approach helped inspire the team behind the scenes. It is a facet that Clutterbuck, a 28-year-old right wing, may not always show publicly.

    “He’s the one talking to us before games, telling us how we have to step up and be successful,” said Cizikas, a fourth-round pick by the Islanders in 2009. “This year he’s coming up with those big goals. That just goes to show how hard he’s working and what he means to this team.”

    Clutterbuck said he and Tavares occasionally reminisced about their junior days for the Generals when their linemate was the prolific Brett MacLean, whose career was cut short by cardiac problems after only 18 N.H.L. games.

    “We talk about it; it was a great time,” Clutterbuck said. “I owe Johnny a lot for my success. It’s a bit nostalgic to be playing with him now.”

    Tavares, a 25-year-old center already in his seventh N.H.L. season, fondly recalled his junior days with Clutterbuck.

    “Pretty much my first two years, Cal was on my line, and it was fun playing together,” said Tavares, who has again played with many linemates this season. “When he’s able to let it rip, it’s tough for goalies. He’s always been a goal scorer.”

    Correction: April 6, 2016

    Because of an editing error, an article on Thursday about Islanders forward Cal Clutterbuck misstated, in some editions, Frans Nielsen’s status with the Islanders during the 2006-7 season, when Clutterbuck and John Tavares were teammates with the Oshawa Generals of the Ontario Hockey League. Nielsen was in his first season with the Islanders, not his second.
