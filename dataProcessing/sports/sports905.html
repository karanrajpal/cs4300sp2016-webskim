    A Late Burst Pushes Seton Hall Past Creighton

    By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 10, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    Isaiah Whitehead had 24 points and 12 rebounds, Khadeen Carrington scored 27, and Seton Hall ran off the final 8 points for an 81-73 victory over pesky Creighton on Thursday night in the quarterfinals of the Big East tournament at Madison Square Garden.

    Carrington put the third-seeded Pirates (23-8) ahead for good with 50 seconds remaining, and Whitehead made several big plays down the stretch before sinking the clinching free throws. Seton Hall will face No. 5 Xavier, the second seed, in the semifinals Friday night.

    Cole Huff had a career-high 35 points for the sixth-seeded Bluejays (18-14), who have lost five of six.

    In the early game, Josh Hart scored 25 points, Ryan Arcidiacono added 19 points and 6 assists, and No. 3 Villanova pulled away from Georgetown (15-18) in the second half to open its bid for consecutive Big East tournament titles with an 81-67 win.

    Kris Jenkins added 15 points for the top-seeded Wildcats (28-4), who will play Providence in the semifinals Friday.

    In a night game, Trevon Bluiett matched his career high with 24 points and Xavier (27-4) dominated from start to finish in a 90-72 victory over Marquette (20-13).

    A.C.C. QUARTERFINALS Looking exhausted down the stretch, No. 19 Duke gave away a lead of 16 points in the second half and lost to Notre Dame, 84-79, in overtime in Washington.

    V. J. Beachem scored 19 points, including a couple of pivotal 3-pointers late in the second half, for fourth-seeded Notre Dame (21-10).

    Grayson Allen led Duke (23-10) with 27 points, but he did not get much help, in part because of foul trouble for an already thin rotation.

    In an earlier quarterfinal game, No. 7 North Carolina got off to a fast start in the tournament as it routed Pittsburgh, 88-71, to take the first step toward its first conference tournament title since 2008.

    Four players scored in double figures as the Tar Heels (26-6) shot 58.9 percent from the field and pulled away from Pittsburgh (20-11) in front of a boisterous crowd in baby blue.

    In a night game, Virginia used its typically stingy defense, plus 26 points from the conference player of the year, Malcolm Brogdon, to move into the semifinals by beating Georgia Tech, 72-52.

    From the start, the Cavaliers (25-6) made things tough for Georgia Tech (19-14), which started 0 for 4 on field-goal attempts, settling for off-balance heaves late in the shot clock. By the end, the Yellow Jackets had 14 turnovers and a shooting percentage of 41 percent.

    In the late game, Sheldon McClellan scored 21 points and Angel Rodriguez had 19 to help No. 11 Miami beat Virginia Tech (19-13) by 88-82. Miami (25-6) will play Virginia in the semifinals.

    ILLINOIS 68, IOWA 66 Jalen Coleman-Lands scored 17 points and Malcolm Hill broke a tie with a 16-foot jumper with 1 minute 14 seconds to go, helping Illinois fend off No. 20 Iowa’s frantic comeback to secure a stunning upset in the second round of the Big Ten tournament in Indianapolis.

    The Fighting Illini (15-18) won for only the second time in six games — this one after allowing the Hawkeyes (21-10) to score 11 straight points in 98 seconds to tie the score at 66-66 with 1:40 to play.

    BIG 12 QUARTERFINALS Perry Ellis scored 21 points, Frank Mason III added 16 and top-ranked Kansas ran roughshod over Kansas State, 85-63, in the Big 12 tournament in Kansas City, Mo.

    The Jayhawks (28-4) led by 45-30 at halftime before pushing their advantage past 20 for much of the second half and coasting the rest of the way.

    The eighth-seeded Wildcats (17-16) were led by Justin Edwards, who hit five 3-pointers and had 23 points.

    In an earlier quarterfinal game, Taurean Prince had 24 points and 13 rebounds, Rico Gathers added 13 points, and No. 22 Baylor’s baffling zone defense shut down No. 23 Texas in a 75-61 victory.

    The fifth-seeded Bears (22-10) opened a 15-point lead in the first half, then used Coach Scott Drew’s trademark defense to keep the fourth-seeded Longhorns (20-12) from coming all the way back.

    Baylor will play Kansas in Friday night’s semifinals.

    In later games, Devin Williams had 18 points and 11 rebounds to lead a balanced West Virginia attack, and the ninth-ranked Mountaineers (25-7) never trailed in an 86-66 victory over Texas Christian (12-21).

    Buddy Hield scored 39 points, Ryan Spangler made a series of critical plays down the stretch and No. 6 Oklahoma (24-6) beat No. 21 Iowa State (21-11), 79-76.

    PACIFIC-12 QUARTERFINALS Chris Boucher had 19 points and 11 rebounds, and No. 8 Oregon withstood a late flurry to hold off Washington, 83-77, at the Pacific-12 tournament in Las Vegas.

    Top-seeded Oregon (26-6) built an 11-point lead midway through the second half, but Andrew Andrews brought the Huskies back with a quick scoring flurry. Andrews, a senior guard, scored 10 of his 19 points during the run, helping eighth-seeded Washington (18-14) pull to 2 points behind late.

    In a later quarterfinal game, Allonzo Trier scored 23 points, Ryan Anderson had 17 points and 11 rebounds, and No. 15 Arizona held on to beat Colorado, 82-78.

    Arizona (25-7) overwhelmed Colorado (22-11) in the first half before laboring through the second.

    In a night game, Kyle Kuzma broke out of a scoring slump at just the right time, missing one shot while scoring 23 points to help lift No. 12 Utah (25-7) to an 80-72 win over Southern California (21-12).

    THREE COACHES FIRED Less than 24 hours after Nebraska defeated Rutgers in the opening round of the Big Ten tournament, the Scarlet Knights dismissed Coach Eddie Jordan. The ax fell even quicker on St. Louis Coach Jim Crews, who was fired moments after the Billikens lost to George Washington in the Atlantic 10 tournament at Barclays Center. The University of Texas at San Antonio also fired its coach, Brooks Thompson.
