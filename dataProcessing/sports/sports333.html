    D’Angelo Russell Video Sends Lakers to a New Low

    By VICTOR MATHERMARCH 31, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    All happy teams are alike; every unhappy team is unhappy in its own way.

    And, oh brother, are the Lakers finding new ways to be unhappy.

    The apotheosis came on Wednesday night with an uncomfortable news conference at which the rookie D’Angelo Russell apologized for videotaping his teammate Nick Young apparently confessing to cheating on his fiancée.

    Kobe Bryant’s final season in the league has been marred by the Lakers’ play: At 16-59, the team stands above only the putrescent 76ers. Bryant is shooting a career low .355.

    Things looked particularly bad on Monday night when the Lakers managed to lose by 48 to the Jazz. Bryant was 1 for 11 and had a plus/minus of minus-43. Russell, a rare bright spot for the team this season, was also atrocious, going 2 for 11.

    The abysmal performance may have been related to locker room turmoil of an unusual sort.

    As first reported by ESPN, Russell, who has a reputation as being a prankster and immature even for his 20 years, had videotaped Young talking about women he had dated. Unfortunately for Young, he is engaged, to the rapper Iggy Azalea. Unfortunately for everyone, the video somehow found its way to the website Fameolous.com and was posted on the Internet for all to see.

    Continue reading the main story

    Young is watching television during the video and seems to be unaware he is being filmed. After discussing an unnamed 19-year-old, Russell asks about the model and performer Amber Rose, but Young denies an assignation, noting that “She knows my girl.”

    At the end, Russell cackles, “I’m glad you told my video all that.” Young responds with a priceless “Huh?” and the video ends.

    Young, who goes by the colorful sobriquet Swaggy P, has been awful this year and has not been playing of late. But that did not prevent a rift in the locker room over the video, according to reports, with Russell being shunned by teammates who felt he had broken an omerta.

    Iggy Azalea certainly noticed:

    Young has not had much to say on Twitter lately; his most recent post, from Monday, merely says “What’s going on in 2016.” (To which one commenter replies, “Not your wedding.”) Russell’s latest post is a cryptic, “Let’s be real!”

    Before Wednesday night’s game, the Lakers held a news conference at which no one seemed happy to be. Russell apologized repeatedly for making the video. “I feel as sick as possible,” he said. “I wish I could make things better right away, but I can’t.” He said he had no idea how the video wound up on the Internet.

    “What happened is what happened,” said a terse Young.

    Asked if he felt he had put Young’s impending marriage in jeopardy, Russell said, “Honestly, I do.”

    Young said, “I don’t want to get into my personal life right now.”

    There was no shortage of opinions from others. The former N.B.A. player Stephen Jackson, known for punching a fan during a 2004 donnybrook in Detroit, told ESPN: “Snitches get stitches. Old rule. Snitches get stitches.”

    Though Azalea seemed to appreciate Russell’s undercover reporting, Lakers fans did not, and he was roundly booed during Wednesday’s game.

    The odd thing is, the Lakers actually won, beating the Heat, 102-100, in overtime. Bryant was just 1 for 7, but Jordan Clarkson had 26 points and Russell shook off the boos to score 16.

    Perhaps the Lakers have taken the first steps back to happiness.
