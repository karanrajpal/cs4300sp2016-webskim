    Penguins Pour It On Against Islanders

    By ALLAN KREDAAPRIL 2, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    There was plenty of palpable playoff race tension Saturday afternoon on top of the heightened atmosphere present whenever the Pittsburgh Penguins and the Islanders meet.

    That tension was broken less than 13 minutes into the first period, courtesy of the Penguins rookie Oskar Sundqvist, who scored the first goal of his N.H.L. career after a miscommunication between Islanders goaltender Jean-Francois Berube and his defensemen.

    Sundqvist’s teammates then continued to pour pucks into the net on their way to a 5-0 demolition of the Islanders, with the surging Penguins winning for the 11th time in 12 games and clinching a playoff berth for the 10th consecutive season.

    “We’ve worked hard; we’ve earned it,” said the Penguins’ captain, Sidney Crosby, who recorded the 600th assist of his career on a second-period goal by Patric Hornqvist. “We just have to continue to play the same way.”

    The Penguins entered the game at Barclays Center against the Islanders — who had won nine of their last 11 at home — without their starting goaltender, Marc-Andre Fleury, who was out with a concussion. He had received that diagnosis Friday, a day after Pittsburgh defeated Nashville.

    Continue reading the main story

    But his replacement, the rookie Matt Murray, stopped 24 shots for the first shutout of his N.H.L. career as the Penguins continued to steamroller their way to the postseason.

    The loss left the Islanders (42-26-9) in the first wild-card position in the Eastern Conference, 2 points ahead of the Philadelphia Flyers, whom they will face next Sunday in the regular-season finale at Barclays Center.

    The Islanders’ captain, John Tavares, was upset with his team’s subpar effort.

    “It was a big game at an important time of the year, and to play the way we did is unacceptable,” he said. “It’s up to us how bad we want it and where we want to get to.”

    The Islanders face an arduous path in their quest for a playoff berth: They will play the rest of the regular season, and perhaps beyond, without their stalwart defenseman Travis Hamonic, who sustained a lower-body injury in a 4-3 win over Columbus on Thursday. Marek Zidlicky, appearing in only his second game since late February, took Hamonic’s spot in the lineup.

    “I don’t think our secondary guys are doing anything to help us win, and I think the older, veteran guys feel that in a way,” Islanders Coach Jack Capuano said. “But there’s still a lot of trust in that room. They believe in each other. They want to play hard.”

    With the victory, the Penguins (45-25-8) moved to 5 points ahead of the Islanders in the Metropolitan Division.

    Both teams started with a spark in what felt like a colder arena after Islanders players complained in recent days of soft ice at Barclays, their new home this season.

    Sundqvist scored a short-handed goal 12 minutes 41 seconds into the opening period. Berube had slid the puck slowly toward his defensemen, but Sundqvist swooped in to intercept before firing a low shot past the goaltender. Hornqvist and Matt Cullen added goals in the second period, and Phil Kessel and Tom Kuhnhackl completed the scoring in the third.

    In the second period, Islanders forward Nikolay Kulemin tangled with Penguins defenseman Kris Letang. Kulemin was assessed an extra two minutes for slashing, and the Penguins were able to capitalize when Hornqvist tipped in a Justin Schultz slap shot at 8:09.

    Cullen, a 39-year-old center who has been a major factor in Pittsburgh’s resurgence, made the score 3-0 15:30 into the second period.

    The Penguins have played like a different team since Mike Sullivan, a former Rangers assistant, replaced Mike Johnston as coach on Dec. 12. After losing their first four games under Sullivan, the Penguins are 30-11-5 and in position to secure home-ice advantage in the first round of the playoffs.

    A huge part of that success has been Crosby’s spectacular play in recent months. Crosby, a 28-year-old center, leads the team with 81 points, 62 of them since Sullivan became coach, and he trailed only Patrick Kane of Chicago and Jamie Benn of Dallas in the N.H.L.’s scoring race entering Saturday.

    Crosby mused about his latest milestone — 600 assists — a feat previously achieved in Penguins history only by Mario Lemieux and Jaromir Jagr.

    “It means you’ve played with some really good players and played a fairly long time,” Crosby said. “It’s nice to get it out of the way and hopefully get more.”

    Before the game, Capuano spoke of the Islanders’ need to forge ahead despite injuries. In addition to Hamonic, who was also missing in last year’s first-round loss to Washington, the Islanders are missing their top goaltender, Jaroslav Halak, and forward Mikhail Grabovski.

    After the loss, center Frans Nielsen emphasized that injuries were always part of the game but could not be used to rationalize poor play.

    “You can’t use it as an excuse,” he said. “You can’t just feel sorry for yourselves. We had a bad game. We have to be better next time.”

    Berube, who has shared goaltending duties with Thomas Greiss since Halak was injured in a 2-1 win over the Penguins on March 8, lost for the first time in regulation since October. He is 3-2-1 this season.

    “No question he struggled,” Capuano said of Berube. “You have to be mentally strong to bounce back. He’s a professional, and he has to battle through it.”

    Correction: April 10, 2016

    Because of an editing error, an article in some editions last Sunday about the Islanders’ 5-0 loss to the Pittsburgh Penguins misstated, in some copies, the score of the Islanders’ victory over the Columbus Blue Jackets a few days before. It was 4-3, not 4-1.
