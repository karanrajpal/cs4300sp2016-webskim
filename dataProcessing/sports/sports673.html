    Cavaliers Beat Magic to Seal Playoff Berth

    By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 18, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    Kyrie Irving scored 26 points and LeBron James had 18, including a layup and a pair of free throws down the stretch, to help the visiting Cleveland Cavaliers hold off the Orlando Magic, 109-103, on Friday night and clinch a playoff spot.

    Channing Frye came off the bench to make four 3-pointers and score 14 against Orlando, his former team, as the Cavaliers beat the Magic for the 14th consecutive time. A rested James returned to the lineup after sitting out Wednesday’s home victory over Dallas and finished 6 of 15 from the field, with eight assists and seven rebounds.

    Victor Oladipo had a career-high 45 points for Orlando but scored only 6 in the fourth quarter. The last of his six 3-pointers trimmed Cleveland’s lead to 107-103 before James made two free throws to put it away.

    Cleveland led by 63-54 at the half, using a 31-9 run to wipe out an 11-point deficit. Kevin Love had two 3-pointers and a dunk during an 11-0 burst that built Cleveland’s lead to double digits.

    Oladipo scored 24 before halftime and 15 more in the third quarter, when the Magic opened with an 18-7 run to regain the lead and cling to an 86-83 edge going into the fourth.

    But in the end, Oladipo did not get enough offensive help to keep the Cavaliers from improving the best record in the Eastern Conference to 49-19 with a fifth victory in six games.

    WARRIORS 130, MAVERICKS 112 Stephen Curry had several dazzling plays on his way to 31 points, Klay Thompson scored 39, and Golden State tuned up for a showdown in San Antonio with a victory on the road over Dallas.

    In one of many displays that drew roars from the typical swarm of Curry’s fans on the road, Curry stole the ball from Dirk Nowitzki near midcourt before hitting a 35-footer over him at the first-quarter buzzer. In the second quarter, he glanced a 3-pointer off the glass from the corner with Nowitzki again in his face.

    The Warriors made 14 of their first 17 from 3-point range, finishing 22 of 38, and won their seventh straight game. On Saturday night they will try to end the N.B.A.’s longest losing streak on another team’s court — 32 straight at San Antonio, a streak that goes back to Feb. 14, 1997.

    THUNDER 111, 76ERS 97 Russell Westbrook recorded his N.B.A.-leading 13th triple-double with 20 points, 15 rebounds and 10 assists, and Kevin Durant had 26 points and 13 rebounds to lead Oklahoma City to a victory over host Philadelphia.

    Westbrook’s 13 triple-doubles tie Grant Hill (1996-97) and Jason Kidd (2007-8) for the most in a season over the last 25 years.

    Enes Kanter contributed 16 points for the Thunder, who clinched a playoff spot with their 14th straight victory over Philadelphia.

    RAPTORS 105, CELTICS 91 Kyle Lowry scored 32 points, Luis Scola had all of his 17 in the first quarter, and host Toronto beat Boston.

    Lowry was 9 for 14 from the field, including 5 of 8 from 3-point range, and added five assists and four rebounds as Toronto led the entire way in its third straight victory.

    PISTONS 115, KINGS 108 Marcus Morris scored 20 of his 24 points in the first half, and Kentavious Caldwell-Pope made big 3-pointers in the fourth quarter to lift host Detroit over Sacramento.

    The Pistons (35-34) pulled into a virtual tie with Chicago (34-33) for the last playoff spot in the Eastern Conference. Detroit is in the middle of a nine-game homestand, and the Pistons can take a small step toward their first postseason berth since 2009 by simply winning home games against struggling teams such as the Kings.

    TRAIL BLAZERS 117, PELICANS 112 Damian Lillard scored 33 points, getting up from a vicious forearm by Kendrick Perkins to help visiting Portland recover from a blown 20-point lead and finish off a feisty New Orleans.

    The Pelicans’ Anthony Davis sat out the second half with a left knee injury, the severity of which was unclear.

    ROCKETS 116, TIMBERWOLVES 111 James Harden had 29 points and 14 assists helping Houston beat visiting Minnesota.
