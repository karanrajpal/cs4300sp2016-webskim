    Ingram Is Latest One-and-Done Blue Devil

    By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAPRIL 4, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    The Duke freshman Brandon Ingram said Monday that he was turning professional, becoming the seventh Blue Devil since 2011 to declare for the N.B.A. draft after one year in college.

    Ingram, 18, announced the move, which had been expected, in a first-person article posted online at The Players’ Tribune. He is widely projected to be one of the first two players, along with Ben Simmons of Louisiana State, to be taken in the June draft.

    Duke’s run of so-called one-and-done players began five years ago with Kyrie Irving, an eventual N.B.A. rookie of the year, and continued last year when three freshmen entered the draft after helping the program win its fifth national title.

    Ingram, a 6-foot-9 swingman from Kinston, N.C., averaged 17.3 points a game this season, matching the third-highest average by a Blue Devils freshman.

    “On one hand, one year at Duke isn’t a very long time,” Ingram, the Atlantic Coast Conference’s freshman of the year, wrote in the article. “I know that. But I’ve done a lot of growing, and growing up, in that time.”

    MINNESOTA GUARD TO TRANSFER Kevin Dorsey, one of three Minnesota guards suspended late in the season after a sex video appeared briefly on social media, plans to transfer to another university.

    Dorsey and the other two players, Nate Mason and Dupree McBrayer, were disciplined after the video appeared on Dorsey’s Twitter account. They missed Minnesota’s final three games of the regular season and the Golden Gophers’ lone game in the Big Ten tournament, putting a dispiriting cap on a season in which the team went 2-16 in the conference.

    The university reinstated the players last month and said they would be eligible to return to the team in good standing at the start of next season.

    Mason and McBrayer have not publicly expressed their plans for next season.

    SOUTH CAROLINA DISMISSES TWO The suspended South Carolina players Jamall Gregory and Eric Cobb were dismissed from the team.

    Gregory and Cobb were arrested in March and charged with misdemeanors in connection with shooting a BB gun. Cobb was accused of firing at an occupied car. Gregory was charged with four counts of property crimes.

    DEAL FOR ILLINOIS PLAYER Prosecutors and a lawyer for the Illinois sophomore Leron Black agreed to a deal that would place him in a diversionary program and potentially have his felony aggravated assault charge reduced to a misdemeanor, allowing him to avoid jail time, said Julia Rietz, the state’s attorney for Champaign County.

    Black was accused of threatening a bouncer at a campus bar with a knife on Feb. 19.

    ARCHIE DEES, INDIANA STAR, DIES Archie Dees, a former Indiana star and the first two-time Big Ten Conference most valuable player, died at 80.

    The death was announced on Facebook by his daughter, Lori Dees Johnson, and confirmed by university officials.

    Dees, a native of Ethel, Miss., averaged 25.4 points and 14.4 rebounds a game in the 1956-57 season, leading the Hoosiers to a Big Ten title and winning his first conference M.V.P. award. The next season, he averaged 25.5 points and 14.4 rebounds en route to another conference crown and M.V.P. honor.

    He was selected by the Cincinnati Royals with the No. 2 overall pick in the 1958 N.B.A. draft and played four seasons in the league, with the Royals, the Detroit Pistons, the Chicago Packers and the St. Louis Hawks. He also spent one season with the Cleveland Pipers of the American Basketball League.

    Dees was inducted into Indiana’s Athletics Hall of Fame in 1983 and was named to the Hoosiers’ all-century team in 2001.

    UCONN’S STEWART FORESEES A WIN Breanna Stewart, on the verge of her fourth straight national title with Connecticut, expressed confidence heading into Tuesday’s final against Syracuse.

    “I’m not going to go and say that we’re going to lose,” Stewart said Sunday night after the Huskies beat Oregon State in the Final Four. “To end my college career, to end it with the other seniors, there is no other way that I want that to happen.”

    Syracuse Coach Quentin Hillsman was not planning to use the comments as extra motivation for his team.

    “I don’t think she knew who she was playing yet,” he said, laughing. “So what is she supposed to say? I told our fans and our crowd that we’re going to win, too.”

    A PERFECT DIVISION II CHAMPION Kelsey Hoppel scored 27 points to help Lubbock Christian defeat Alaska-Anchorage, 78-73, in Indianapolis for the Division II championship in the team’s first season of full N.C.A.A. eligibility.

    Nicole Hampton had 22 points, 13 rebounds, 9 assists and 7 steals for the Lady Chaps (35-0), the third Division II women’s basketball team to complete a perfect season.
