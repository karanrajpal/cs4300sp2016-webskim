    Horse Racing

    The Kentucky Derby Dartboard: Week 2

    APRIL 10, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    Top contenders for the 142nd running of the Kentucky Derby are listed in order of preference, with comments provided by Joe Drape and Melissa Hoppert of The New York Times. Churchill Downs has adopted a points system to determine the Derby field, with points being earned by the top four finishers in designated prep races. The odds are from the Churchill Downs futures pool, and the records are starts-win-place-show. An article on Saturday’s Santa Anita Derby, Wood Memorial and Bluegrass Stakes can be found here.

    1. Nyquist

    Trainer: Doug O’Neill Jockey: Mario Gutierrez

    Record: 7-7-0-0 Points: 130 Odds: 3-1

    J.D.: The 2-year-old champion is the one to beat. He is also undefeated. Is he bred for a mile and a quarter? No, but few are.

    M.H.: This Florida Derby winner is the clear Derby favorite, and who else might hit the board is a guessing game at this point.

    Next up: Kentucky Derby on May 7.

    2. Lani

    Trainer: Mikio Matsunaga Jockey: Yutaka Take

    Record: 6-3-1-0 Points: 100 Odds: 25-1

    J.D.: This Kentucky-bred, Japan-based colt stood out in the U.A.E. Derby. None of the American horses look anywhere close to Nyquist. Why not take a flier with this grandson of Sunday Silence?

    Continue reading the main story

    M.H.: This Tapit colt has three wins in his last four races and is already at Churchill Downs training.

    Next up: Kentucky Derby.

    3. Cupid

    Trainer: Bob Baffert Jockey: Martin Garcia

    Record: 4-2-1-0 Points: 50 Odds: 11-1

    J.D.: The Arkansas Derby will indicate if this is the best arrow in Bob Baffert’s quiver. Cupid is a free-running colt but also a lightly raced one. He needs to learn fast.

    M.H.: This Tapit colt overcame a troubled trip to win the Rebel Stakes, and if he wins at Oaklawn Park, he could steal the thunder from his stablemate Mor Spirit.

    Next up: Arkansas Derby on Saturday.

    4. Exaggerator

    Trainer: Keith Desormeaux Jockey: Kent Desormeaux

    Record: 9-4-2-1 Points: 126 Odds: 27-1

    J.D.: He seemingly rose from the dead, running away with the Santa Anita Derby after a disappointing third-place finish in his previous race. This colt has some back class.

    M.H.: This Curlin colt has more races under his belt than any other horse on our list. That experience could help come Derby day, but are the glory days of 46-year-old Kent Desormeaux behind him?

    Next up: Kentucky Derby.

    5. Mohaymen

    Trainer: Kiaran McLaughlin Jockey: Junior Alvarado

    Record: 6-5-0-0 Points: 80 Odds: 8-1

    Advertisement

    Continue reading the main story

    J.D.: This colt went from Wonder Horse to What the Heck Happened with an awful performance in the Florida Derby. Do you draw a line through it? Nope.

    M.H.: He had a tough trip on a wet track in the Florida Derby, going wide throughout. Now the question becomes, can he regain his form in the Derby and show that that was just an off day, or will he continue to regress?

    Next up: Kentucky Derby.

    6. Mor Spirit

    Trainer: Bob Baffert Jockey: Gary Stevens

    Record: 7-3-4-0 Points: 84 Odds: 11-1

    J.D.: This Pennsylvania-bred colt is as steady as they come, but he looked ordinary clunking up for second in the Santa Anita Derby.

    M.H.: This speedy colt has been high on Baffert’s list since Day 1, and with his recent track record, my money is on him to finish second on the first Saturday in May.

    Next up: Kentucky Derby.

    7. Destin

    Trainer: Todd Pletcher Jockey: Javier Castellano

    Record: 5-3-1-0 Points: 51 Odds: 16-1

    J.D.: He won the Sam F. Davis Stakes and the Tampa Bay Derby, but against whom exactly?

    M.H.: He set a track record for a mile and a sixteenth while outdueling his stablemate Outwork in the Tampa Bay Derby. Then Outwork went on to win the Wood Memorial, which adds intrigue.

    Next up: Kentucky Derby.

    8. Gun Runner

    Trainer: Steve Asmussen Jockey: Florent Geroux

    Record: 5-4-0-0 Points: 151 Odds: 13-1

    Advertisement

    Continue reading the main story

    J.D.: Meet your West Virginia Derby winner.

    M.H.: An athletic colt with tactical speed, he swept the two big Derby prep races at the Fair Grounds in New Orleans. But he hardly seems fast enough to win the Derby.

    Next up: Kentucky Derby.

    9. Outwork

    Trainer: Todd Pletcher Jockey: John Velazquez

    Record: 4-3-1-0 Points: 120 Odds: 30-1

    J.D.: He won the Wood Memorial — albeit by a head in the slowest winning time in the history of the race. He is lightly raced, though, and getting better.

    M.H.: He could eventually be formidable but not in time to don roses at the Derby.

    Next up: Kentucky Derby.

    10. Mo Tom

    Trainer: Tom Amoss Jockey: Corey Lanerie

    Record: 7-3-0-3 Points: 32 Odds: 17-1

    J.D.: This talented colt has been buried by terrible rides in his last two races, so look for a rider switch now that Mike Smith and a couple of others appear available. He is a live long shot.

    M.H.: The Saints owners Tom and Gayle Benson have two Derby contenders in Mo Tom and Tom’s Ready. Both are already at Churchill Downs, which is a bonus.

    Next up: Kentucky Derby.
