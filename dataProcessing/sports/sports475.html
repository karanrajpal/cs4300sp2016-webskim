    Spurs Roll to 37-0 at Home, Tying a Season-Opening Mark

    By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMARCH 26, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    LaMarcus Aldridge had 32 points and 12 rebounds, and the San Antonio Spurs defeated the Memphis Grizzlies, 110-104, on Friday night to match the best home start in league history with their 37th in a row.

    San Antonio’s streak tied the season-opening mark set by the Chicago Bulls in 1995-96 during their record 72-victory season. The Spurs have won 46 consecutive regular-season games at home, dating to March 2015.

    The Spurs won with Kawhi Leonard missing the game with a bruised right quadriceps and Danny Green, Boris Diaw and Patty Mills sitting out for rest. The starters Marc Gasol, Zach Randolph and Mike Conley were among the missing for the Grizzlies.

    Kevin Martin had a season-high 13 points for the Spurs in his first start since signing with San Antonio on March 9.

    JaMychal Green had 20 points to lead Memphis, which has lost two straight.

    PISTONS, 112, HORNETS 105 Andre Drummond had 18 points and 14 rebounds, and host Detroit overwhelmed Charlotte with the Pistons’ highest-scoring first half of the season on the way to a victory.

    Continue reading the main story

    Detroit led by 72-56 after two quarters and won its fifth straight game, taking a two-game lead over Chicago for the eighth and final playoff spot in the Eastern Conference. The Pistons also pulled within a percentage point of seventh-place Indiana.

    Kemba Walker scored 25 of his 29 points in the first half for Charlotte.

    Marcus Morris scored 19 of his 20 points in the first half for Detroit (39-34), and Kentavious Caldwell-Pope scored 16 of his 21 in the second half. Reggie Jackson added 17 points for the Pistons.

    HAWKS 101, BUCKS 90 Jeff Teague scored 12 of his 18 points in the fourth quarter as host Atlanta overcame poor 3-point shooting to beat Milwaukee.

    Paul Millsap had 14 points and 13 rebounds, and Al Horford also had 14 points to keep the Hawks’ late-season surge going. They have won seven of eight and 10 of 12 to move to third place in the Eastern Conference.

    The Hawks (43-30) are a season-high 13 games over .500 and won even though they made only 5 of 32 3-pointers.

    Jabari Parker led Milwaukee with 19 points. John Henson had 17 points and 10 rebounds.

    ROCKETS 112, RAPTORS 109 James Harden had his third triple-double this season with 32 points, 13 assists and 11 rebounds to help host Houston end a three-game skid with a win over Toronto.

    Houston squandered a double-digit lead early but opened the fourth quarter with a big run to take control.

    In the last few minutes, the Toronto stars DeMar DeRozan and Kyle Lowry were ejected for arguing with the officials.

    DeRozan scored 18 points before being kicked out with about three minutes left.

    HEAT 108, MAGIC 97 Hassan Whiteside scored 26 points, grabbed 12 rebounds and blocked 5 shots to lead Miami over visiting Orlando.

    Goran Dragic scored 22 points and handed out 8 assists for Miami, which is 14-1 in its last 15 games against the Magic.

    The rookie Josh Richardson scored 14, Amare Stoudemire had 13, Dwyane Wade finished with 11, and Luol Deng had a 10-point, 13-rebound night for the Heat.

    In his last 18 games, all off the bench, Whiteside is averaging 18 points and 13.7 rebounds on 60.5 percent shooting — with 16 double-doubles.

    T’WOLVES 132, WIZARDS 129 Karl-Anthony Towns scored 27 points and grabbed 10 rebounds, and Minnesota dealt a blow to host Washington’s playoff hopes with a double-overtime victory.

    Zach LaVine added 25 points, and Gorgui Dieng scored 18 while helping the Timberwolves to comebacks at the end of regulation and the second overtime.

    Bradley Beal scored 26 points as the 10th-place Wizards fell three and a half games behind Detroit for the Eastern Conference’s eighth playoff spot.
