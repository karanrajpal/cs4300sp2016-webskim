    You will always have that love in your heart.

    I wish to tell parents of stillborn angels that, no matter what anyone says (your "well meaning" family members, society, etc.) you are still a parent, and you will always be. The grief you feel is overwhelming, but remember that for a moment (no matter how fleeting) you knew the joy of giving birth to your baby, that you got to hold him or her, to see how beautiful they were, who they looked like the most. You will always have that love in your heart.

    Also, be gentle on yourselves, with your grieving. You have been through a life changing experience -- the momentous joy of a child’s birth, and the raw, open wound of your baby’s death. Don’t feel that you have to grieve “on schedule,” and that you should be “done” with your grief after a certain number of months or years. Don’t let the “well-meant” rush you, scold you, or ask questions like “You’re STILL not over it?” Your child was a life. Your child was REAL. You don’t just heal from death as if it was a bad cut, or a bad case of stomach flu. Let yourself feel your emotions -- your love, your sadness, your anger, your rage. There will be times (especially in the first days) where you can’t even think about getting out of bed. When my son passed away, the days without him dragged on. My life -- the life I had prepared with a child to see grow up, the life we would share -- seemed instead feels like a life sentence. I can never say it gets easier, but you take the days after your child’s death one day at a time.
